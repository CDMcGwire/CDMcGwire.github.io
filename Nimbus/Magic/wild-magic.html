Wild Magic

A very special trait

How it Works

If you have the Wild Magic trait, you will roll an extra d20 whevenever you cast a spell or whenever any sort of magic first effects you. If a natural 20 is rolled, you will determine what kind of effect occurs using the following table.

The Wild Magic trait exists to give players the option to introduce a touch of chaos into the game. There is a 5% chance (a natural 20 on a d20 roll), that any magic effecting or cast from a player will result in some random effect. This section explains the effect generation process.

Determining Base Effect

To determine what effect occurs, roll another d20 then match the number to this table:

  • 1: The effect results in some catastrophic event for the party. Roll the Doom table!
  • 2: The effect dies out completely; If it was from an enchanted item, the item loses its enchantment.
  • 3: The effect explodes centered on the source! Roll on the Explosion table below.
  • 4: The effect explodes centered on the target! Roll on the Explosion table.
  • 5: The effect happens at 1/100th speed. Power stays the same, It's just... slow.
  • 6: Something is summoned... Roll on the Summoning Table.
  • 7: The effect is inverted! The source becomes the target. If the source was an enchanted object, roll on the Feedback table.
  • 8: Any rolls associated with the effect are criticals. Flip a coin to determine if it's a Critical Success or Failure.
  • 9: Something weird happens... Roll the Weird table.
  • 9: Something weird happens... Roll the Weird table.
