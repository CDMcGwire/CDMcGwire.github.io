Wild Magic

A very special trait

How it Works

If you have the Wild Magic trait, you will roll an extra d20 whevenever you cast a spell or whenever any sort of magic first effects you. If a natural 20 is rolled, you will determine what kind of effect occurs using the following table.

The Wild Magic trait exists to give players the option to introduce a touch of chaos into the game. There is a 5% chance (a natural 20 on a d20 roll), that any magic effecting or cast from a player will result in some random effect. This section explains the effect generation process.

Determining Base Effect

To determine what effect occurs, roll another d20 then match the number to this table:

  • 1: The effect dies out completely; If it was from an enchanted item, the item loses its enchantment.
  • 2-5: The effect explodes centered on the source! Roll on the Explosion table below.
  • 6: The effect results in some catastrophic event for the party. Roll the Doom table!
  • 7: Any rolls associated with the effect are criticals. Flip a coin to determine if it's a Critical Success or Failure.
  • 8-10: The effect explodes centered on the target! Roll on the Explosion table.
  • 11: The effect happens at 1/100th speed. Power stays the same, It's just... slow.
  • 12: Something is summoned... Roll on the Summoning Table.
  • 13: The effect is inverted! The source becomes the target. If the source was an enchanted object, roll on the Feedback table.
  • 14: Every living thing within 60 feet of the effect's target must make an Intellect roll with a -2 penalty, or it is sent into a state of primal fear. The affected being may make the same roll once per turn to end the effect.
  • 15: Something weird happens... Roll the Weird table.
  • 16-18: The effect becomes some other effect. Roll the Random Effect table.
  • 19: Every living creature within 60 feet of the effect's target has a sudden bout of clumsiness. Any held items are dropped, and all affected must make an Agility roll with a -2 penalty, or they will fall to the ground.
  • 20: The effect results in something greatly beneficial to the party! Roll the Deus Ex Machina table.
Deus Ex Machina Table

Roll a d6 for this table:

  • 1: Everyone in the party is healed 1d10 hit points
  • 2: Everyone in the party gains a +5 to their next attack
  • 3: Everyone in the party gains an extra 1d6 on their next damage roll
  • 4: Everyone in the party is fully healed
  • 5: Everyone in the party may move themselves up to 30 feet in any direction; No effects are triggered by this movement
  • 6: The party may designate one enemy to deal 5d10 damage to or they may choose to remove d6 enemies with 20 or less health
Doom Table

Roll a d6 for this table:

  • 1: Everyone in the party falls over
  • 2: Everyone in the party takes an immediate 1d10 damage
  • 3: Everyone in the party suffers a -5 on their next attack
  • 4: Everyone in the party is blinded for 2 turns
  • 5: All enemies in the area are healed 1d12
  • 6: A random party member explodes violently and horrificaly
Explosion Table

To start, roll a d6 to determine the size of the blast radius. Multiply the die result by 5 to get the radius in feet.

Next, roll another d6 to determine the explosion type:

  • 1: Weak explosion; Creatures in the area must pass an Agility roll else take 1d6 damage
  • 2: Fireball; objects in the area light on fire. Creatures must pass an Agility roll or catch on fire.
  • 3: Arcane; Creatures in the area take 1d8 magical damage
  • 4: Flash; Creatures in the area must pass an Agility roll or are blinded for 2 turns
  • 5: Massive force; All creatures in the area must pass a Brawn roll or are pushed 10 feet
  • 6: Massive explosion; 2d10 damage to all in radius. Creatures may make an Agility roll to halve the damage
