Weapons

Hack, slash, and stab

Weapon Categories

Conventional weapons belong groups based on general category. These groups give the base stats for a weapon of that type, which specific variations may or may not modify. For instance, an arming sword acts as a pretty standard "Long Blade," with no extra stat bonuses. A rapier, however, would have slightly reduced damage and the armor piercing trait.

Melee Weapons

Melee weapon categories are further split into those that can be used with off-hand items, like shields, and those that can't. Specific variations may override.

  • Can be used with off-hand item
    • Axes: 1d10 damage; -1 to-hit; Blow-Through
    • Short Blades: 1d4+3 damage
    • Long Blades: 1d8+1 damage; +1 to-hit
    • Clubs: 1d6 damage; Knockout; Blow-Through
    • Maces: 1d8 damage; Blow-Through
    • Flails: 1d8+2 damage; -2 to-hit; Blow-Through
    • Spears: 1d8+1 damage; 10-foot range;
    • Unarmed: See section from "Attacking and Defense"
  • Cannot be used with off-hand item
    • Two-Handed Blades: 2d6 damage; -1 to-hit; Blow-Through
    • Polearms: 1d12 damage; -1 to-hit; 10-foot range
Ranged Weapons

Ranged weapons have effective ranges, which are noted in their list entry. These declare what range you can expect to deal full damage at. Shooting outside of effective range decreases accuracy and damage by -2 for each range length passed.

For example, A ranged weapon with with of 120 feet will have a -2 from 121 to 240 feet, and a -4 from 241 to 360 feet.

  • Bows: 1d8 damage; Range of 180 feet
  • Crossbows: 1d8+2 damage; +1 to-hit; Range of 120 feet; Slow Reload
  • Throwing Knives: 1d4 damage; 60 feet

Slow Reload

Weapons with this trait require a move action to reload.
