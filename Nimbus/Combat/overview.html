Combat Basics

How to hit things with sticks
The Basic Process

At certain points during an adventure your party will find themselve in an all out brawl of some sort. Combat is handled in a turn based fashion, where each involved character rolls initiative to determine order, then they precede to take actions one at a time. A full set of turns is a Round and it represents around 5 to 6 seconds.

The process is thus:

  • Combat encounter is formally announced by the GM.
  • Initiative is rolled.
  • All participants take turns making an action and/or move.
  • When the round is over, reroll initiative.
  • Repeat last two steps until the GM deems the encounter "over"
The Encounter

An encounter starts when a player character is involved combat, whether they start it or they get dragged into it. An encounter also starts when the GM says it does. Simple as that.

The encounter ends when all hostility ends, or the GM says it ends. An encounter consists as a series of rounds in which each involved character take turns to perform actions in an attempt to resolve hostilities.

Initiative

Initiative is based off the rolling character or NPC’s Speed attribute.

The Roll: d20 + (2 x Agility)

The highest roller goes first. Ties go to the one with a higher base Agility, then to who has a higher base Perception, then a roll-off if it keeps going.

Your Turn

On your turn, you may move and take an action, possibly multiple small action if your GM allows. A turn represents approximately 5 seconds of real world time.

Movement

Base Movement Speed = 30 (in game) feet per turn + 5 feet for every 2 points of Agility above 0. On a square grid, 5’ equals one square. On a hex grid, it is equal to one hex. If no grid is used, 5’ in game is one real-life inch.

During your turn, you may forgo an action to sprint, thus doubling your movement that turn. Ranged attacks suffer a -2 to hit a sprinting target. However, sprinting allows characters in melee range to take an Attack of Opportunity as described later.

Impaired Movement

If the GM declares your movement impaired, your move speed is halved. If the GM declares you heavily impaired, you can move no more than 5 feet per turn.

Common causes of impairment are: Climbing, walking on uneven surfaces, injured legs, and the like.

An "Action"

Things that are hard ruled as taking an action:

  • Making a single attack
  • Grabbing an item (or person)
  • Opening a door/window
  • Throwing an item (or person)
  • Casting a spell
  • Smashing or lifting objects

Spell casting can vary in how long it takes for the spell to finish. This will be stated in the spell’s description. If unstated, it’s a single turn.

An unwritten action is up to the GM’s discretion.

Some actions don’t require much time and can be slipped into a standard round of action. Such as saying, “I look at the window.” Looking is an action, but a single glance takes next to no time. If you say examine the window, that’s a full action as you are closely studying the window and taking time to note the details.

Generally, a player may do 1 or 2 quick actions in their turn, depending on the action taken (once again, up to the GM’s discretion). Any more than that, and it takes a full action.
