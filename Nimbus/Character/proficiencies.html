Proficiencies

What makes you a professional

What are they?

Proficiencies allow for a character to specialize beyond just their attributes in specific groups of actions. While they get base bonuses from attributes, they arenâ€™t limited by attributes. Investing training into different skills grants bonuses to rolls proportional to the rank of proficiency.

These are not intended to be comprehensive for all of the skills associated with adventuring. Instead, these represent tasks that usually require a good deal of prior training, experience, or natural talent before one can hope to be consistently successful at these tasks in stressful circumstances.

Determining Proficiency

To train a skill you invest proficiency points. When creating your character, you start with 3 combat proficiency points and 4 non-combat points. The bonuses from combat proficiencies depend on the type. Non-combat proficiencies give a +1 to their rolls per rank.

Attribute Bonus

Each proficiency is linked to an attribute. This means that the total bonus when rolling for a skill is equal to your bonus in the linked attribute + your proficiency. A proficiency may have multiple attributes attributed to it. In most cases, you will use the higher bonus of the two.
