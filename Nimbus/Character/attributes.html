Attributes

The core of your character

Attribute Bonuses

There are a few common rolls that are based on specific attributes. Those are referenced here. But otherwise, specific rules will explain themselves.

Health

Your starting health is equal to 12 + (2 x Brawn Bonus)

Determining Your Attributes

  • All stats begin at 0
  • You may then assign 5 points
  • Each point spent is a +1 bonus to any rolls made with that attribute
  • You can take away 1 point from any attribute to gain an extra point in another attribute
  • An attribute may not start lower than -4
  • An attribute may not start higher than +4

Attributes may of course pass the initial limits through leveling or other in-game events.

Note: While a +8 is very large bonus, a -6 to an attribute can often leave your character to be very vulnerable in a variety of situations. A -2, however, is often worth taking to reinforce a particular strength, as the penalty is small enough that it won’t be a huge weakness, and party members can make up for what you lack in that area.

Agility

Agility represents your character’s physical speed and precision. It influences success in any task that requires a deft touch or reflexive action. Such as picking a pocket, disarming a trap, or dodging an arrow.

Agility is used directly in:

  • Defense score
  • Initiative bonus
  • Damage for some weapons
Brawn

Brawn represents your character’s physical strength and endurance. It influences success in any task that requires brute force or sustained physical effort. Such as breaking down a door or holding heavy objects for an extended time.

Brawn is used directly in:

  • Most melee damage
  • Breaking and lifting objects
  • Health and endurance rolls
Charisma

Charisma represents your character’s natural charm and social magnetism. It influences success in any task that requires influencing another being. Such as persuading a noble or leading an army.

Most uses of Charisma are directly related to some proficiency.

Intellect

Intellect represents your character’s mental capacity and reasoning ability. It influences success in any task that requires memorization or some sort of calculation. Such as reciting a poem, casting a spell, or decrypting a code.

Intellect is often used directly in:

  • Magical Defense
  • Mental endurance
Perception

Perception represents your character’s sensory attunement. It influences success in any task that require some sort of detection. Such as gauging someone’s mood, spotting small objects, or smelling dangerous fumes.

Perception is used directly in:

  • Passive awareness tests

Example Builds

Knight

  • Agility: 0
  • Brawn: +4
  • Charisma: +1
  • Intellect: 0
  • Perception: 0

Bard

  • Agility: +2
  • Brawn: -2
  • Charisma: +3
  • Intellect: +1
  • Perception: +1
