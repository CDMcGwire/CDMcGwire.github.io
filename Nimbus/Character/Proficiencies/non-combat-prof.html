Non-Combat Proficiencies

Problem solving without weapons
Knowledge

Arcana

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to recall and absorb information about the magical and otherworldly. For example, determining the weakness of an elemental entity.

History

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to absorb and recollect historical information. For example, recalling that the ancient elven ruins they are in was the site of a great sacrifice.

Medicine

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to learn and use knowledge of a medical nature. Such as knowing what type of plant helps relieve pain.

Politics

Intellect or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to learn and recall information about the rules and rulers of societies. For example, knowing the penalty for potato theft and who will serve as judge in your trial.

Street Wisdom

Charisma or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to pick up on current knowledge in a community. Such as knowing who’s in charge of the local guard post and what the patrol routes are.

Mechanical

Arcane Devices

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to craft and manipulate devices of the magical variety. For example, imbuing and using a wand.

Crafting

Agility

Represents your character’s ability to assemble materials into simple machines and items, such as makeshift ovens and medical kits. For example, building a chair or repairing a door.

Devices

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to work with intricate devices, such as clocks, crossbow trigger mechanisms, or the like. For example, solving a puzzle box.

Smithing

Brawn

Represents your character’s ability to work a forge, creating and modifying metalwork. For example, creating a sword or fixing armor.

Movement

Acrobatics

Agility

Represents your character’s ability to perform difficult maneuvers, like high jumps and balancing on a pole. For example, reducing damage when falling from a tall building.

Climbing

Brawn or Agility

Represents your character’s ability to find hand holds and endure hanging for prolonged time. For example, scaling a 100-foot tower or getting over a smooth wall.

Swimming

Brawn

Represents your character’s ability to maneuver in liquid. For example, swimming in a river a mile upstream.

Social

Bartering

Charisma or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to buy and sell in their favor. For example, selling a diamond for twice its market worth.

Charm

Charisma

Represents your character’s ability to gain favor and influence with others through personal magnetism and a quick tongue. For example, convincing the bartender to give you a free drink.

Deception

Charisma or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to convince others of whatever they wish. Whether through lying, visual, or auditory tricks. For example, tricking a man into thinking his house is haunted.

Intimidation

Brawn

Represents your character’s ability to showcase his power to scare off, pacify, or bully others. For example, scattering a group of wolves by bellowing and waving your sword.

Performance

Agility or Charisma

Represents your character’s ability to put on a show. For example, dancing for money.

Persuasion

Intellect or Perception

Represents using reasoning to influence others to see your way. For example, convincing a noble that some architectural schematic would be too costly to build.

Subterfuge

Make Forgery

Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to create a convincing fake. For example, creating a document of free passage into a city.

Sleight of Hand

Agility

Represents your character’s ability to grab or manipulate objects and go unnoticed by watchful eyes. For example, stealing a wallet from someone’s pocket.

Stealth

Agility or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to sneak around. For example, hiding behind a table when the owner of the store you are stealing from walks back into the room.

Traps

Agility or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to setup and detect physical traps. For example, setting up a trip-wire or disarming an improvised explosive.

Survival

First Aid

Agility or Intellect

Represents your character’s ability to treat wounds. Such as stopping the bleeding from a missing leg.

Gather Food

Agility or Perception

Represents your character’s ability to scavenge food in the wilderness or where supplies are short. For example, collecting berries.
